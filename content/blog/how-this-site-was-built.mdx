I rebuilt my portfolio from scratch this year. Not because the old one was broken, but because I wanted something that felt like mine. Not a template. Not a Figma export. Something where every detail was a decision I could explain.

This is that explanation.

## The pixel font

The biggest risk on this site is the typography. I use Geist Pixel Square as the primary structural typeface for headings, navigation, labels, buttons, section titles. Everything structural is pixel.

Nobody in the designer portfolio space does this. Most portfolios use Inter or some safe geometric sans. I wanted something unexpected, something that says "this person thinks about typography differently." Pixel fonts feel technical and retro-futuristic at the same time. They force you to be precise because every character is visible at that resolution.

Geist Sans handles the body text. Paragraphs, descriptions, anything you need to actually read. Pixel is for identity, not reading. That distinction matters.

There's also a third font. A handwritten face called HB Handscript that appears exactly once, in the hero. "A little page on the internet all about me." One moment of warmth in an otherwise precise system.

## No pure white, no pure black

The background is `#f2f0ec`. Not white. Warm off-white. The text is `#3f4f5c`. Not black. Slate blue. This was a rule from day one: no pure white, no pure black, anywhere in the UI.

The reason is simple. Pure white is harsh. Pure black is heavy. Warm neutrals feel like paper. The whole palette is built on this idea. There's a warm off-white canvas, slightly lighter card panels, and a dusty rose accent that ties everything together.

## Seasonal accents

The accent color changes with the season.

Spring is dusty rose. Summer shifts to warm amber. Autumn goes burnt sienna. Winter is slate blue. The site literally feels different depending on when you visit it.

I think about this like tending a garden. The site is alive. It responds to time. Most portfolios are static. This one breathes. It's a small thing but it changes the relationship between the visitor and the site. You come back in October and the accent is different. That's memorable.

## Sound design

The site has sound. Soft clicks on navigation, gentle tones on hover, a chime for the snake easter egg. All opt-in. Muted by default. Sound only activates after you interact.

I added this because I believe interfaces can be multi-sensory. A wooden click when you navigate feels different than silence. It's not about being flashy. It's about craft. The sounds are quiet and organic, like tapping a ceramic bowl.

Web haptics pair with the sounds on supported devices. A subtle vibration on a button press. These things compound.

## Springs, not easing

Every animation uses spring physics. Not `ease-in-out`. Springs. The motion library has three core presets: a soft ease for entrances, an exit curve, and a smooth spring for interactive elements. Every animation in the codebase references these constants.

The stagger pattern follows what I call the storyboard approach. Named timing constants at the top of the file. A single integer state variable drives the sequence. Config objects define initial and final values. It reads like a script: at 60ms the profile fades in, at 100ms the text panel glides up, at 220ms the intro paragraph rises.

`prefers-reduced-motion` disables everything. Animation should never be the only way to understand the interface.

## Shadows over borders

Cards don't use borders. They use multi-layer box shadows. Three layers in light mode: a 1px pseudo-border at 6% opacity, a subtle lift shadow, and a soft ambient shadow. Hover deepens all three.

Dark mode flips the approach. Deeper shadows plus an inset `rgba(255,255,255,0.04)` for inner edge definition. The same card works in both modes without conditional logic.

Borders are rigid. Shadows adapt to any background. That's the principle.

## The background has texture

The background isn't flat. Three layers: a warm radial gradient from center-top, a linear paper gradient, and a grain texture at near-invisible opacity. You don't notice any of these individually. Together they create a paper feel.

The hero has its own sky gradient on top. Two radial spot highlights that give the top of the page a sense of atmosphere. Subtle enough that you feel it more than see it.

## What this site is not

Not flashy. No neon gradients. No glassmorphism. No bouncing elements. Not trendy for the sake of being trendy. If something is removed, it was intentional. If it looks default, I revisit it.

The philosophy is warm minimal. Japandi-evolved. When in doubt, do less. Let typography, spacing, and composition create hierarchy. The site should feel like a well-made tool. Precise, warm, and quiet.

That's the whole game.

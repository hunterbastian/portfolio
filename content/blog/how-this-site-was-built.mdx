I rebuilt my portfolio this year because I wanted something that felt like mine. Every detail is a decision I can explain.

## Pixel font

Geist Pixel Square for all structural text. Headings, nav, labels, buttons. Nobody in portfolio-land does this. Most reach for Inter. I wanted unexpected.

Geist Sans for body text. Pixel is for identity, not reading.

A handwritten face appears once, in the hero. One moment of warmth.

## Warm neutrals

Background is `#f2f0ec`. Text is `#3f4f5c`. No pure white, no pure black, anywhere. Warm neutrals feel like paper.

## Seasonal accents

The accent color shifts with the season. Dusty rose in spring, warm amber in summer, burnt sienna in autumn, slate blue in winter. The site responds to time.

## Sound

Soft clicks on navigation. Gentle tones on hover. All opt-in, muted by default. Interfaces can be multi-sensory. The sounds are quiet and organic, like tapping a ceramic bowl.

## Springs

Every animation uses spring physics. Three presets, referenced everywhere. `prefers-reduced-motion` disables all of it.

## Shadows over borders

Cards use multi-layer box shadows, not borders. Shadows adapt to any background. Hover deepens all three layers.

## Texture

The background has three barely-visible layers: a warm radial gradient, a paper gradient, and grain. You feel it more than see it.

## What this site is not

Not flashy. Not trendy. When in doubt, do less.

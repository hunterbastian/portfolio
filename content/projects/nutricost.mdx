<ScrollStage chapter="02" title="The Work">

<ScrollReveal>

The job was label redesigns — not net-new design, but updating existing products to meet current regulatory requirements while refreshing the visual direction. Every label has to balance the same tension: the front needs to sell the product on a crowded Amazon listing, and the back needs to fit FDA-mandated supplement facts, ingredient lists, allergen warnings, and legal disclaimers into a space that doesn't want them.

</ScrollReveal>

<ScrollReveal delay={200}>

The production queue moved fast. Labels came in batches — sometimes a dozen variants of the same product (different flavors, sizes, counts) that all needed to stay visually consistent while each getting individual regulatory review. The creative director set the brand direction; my job was to execute within it and flag anything that didn't work.

</ScrollReveal>

</ScrollStage>

<DesignDecision
  question="How do you handle regulatory text that fights the layout?"
  chosen={0}
  options='[{"label":"Structured hierarchy with clear zones","description":"Supplement labels have non-negotiable FDA requirements — supplement facts panels, ingredient lists, allergen callouts, and disclaimers all need specific sizing and placement. Instead of treating regulatory content as an afterthought that gets squeezed in at the end, I built the back panel layout around it first. Regulatory text gets its own clear zones with consistent type sizing, then the remaining space is what you actually design with. Less creative freedom, but nothing gets missed."},{"label":"Design-first, fit compliance after","description":"Looks better initially but creates a cycle of revision. Every time a regulatory requirement changes or a new ingredient gets added, the layout breaks and needs to be re-worked. Designing around compliance from the start is slower upfront but saves hours on the back end — especially across 100+ SKUs."}]'
/>

<DesignDecision
  question="How do you maintain consistency across hundreds of product variants?"
  chosen={0}
  options='[{"label":"Templatized master files","description":"Each product family shares a master PSD with locked layers for brand elements and editable layers for variant-specific content — flavor name, color accent, product-specific claims. <ScrollStage chapter="04" title="Retrospective">

<ScrollReveal>

This was my first time designing inside someone else's system at production speed. School projects give you a blank canvas and unlimited time. This was the opposite — an established brand, real constraints, and a queue that expected output every day.

</ScrollReveal>

<ScrollReveal delay={200}>

The biggest lesson was that production design is a different discipline than concept design. The creative director wasn't looking for my best ideas — they were looking for consistency, accuracy, and speed. Knowing when to execute within a system and when to push back on it is a skill I didn't have before this internship. The work isn't flashy, but the thinking behind it is real — and it ships.

</ScrollReveal>

</ScrollStage>
